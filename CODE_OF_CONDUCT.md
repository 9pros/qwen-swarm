# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** of differing viewpoints and experiences
- **Gracefully accepting** constructive criticism
- **Focusing** on what is best for the community
- **Showing empathy** towards other community members
- **Being inclusive** and welcoming to newcomers
- **Providing help** and guidance to others
- **Sharing knowledge** and experiences
- **Encouraging** others to contribute
- **Celebrating** success and achievements

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, sexual, racial, or otherwise
- **Trolling**: Deliberate provocation or inflammatory comments
- **Spam**: Unsolicited advertising or repetitive content
- **Personal Attacks**: Insults, ad hominem attacks, or personal criticism
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory language or behavior
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing private information without consent
- **Threats**: Threats of violence or harm
- **Disruption**: Deliberate disruption of discussions or projects
- **Abuse of Power**: Using position or influence unfairly

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community. Examples of representing a project or community include using an official project e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event. Representation of a project may be further defined and clarified by project maintainers.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you believe someone is violating the Code of Conduct, please report it by:

1. **Email**: max@9pros.com
2. **Private Message**: Contact any maintainer directly
3. **GitHub**: Use the "Report abuse" feature on GitHub

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### Investigation Process

1. **Receipt**: You will receive an acknowledgment of your report within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: The report will be investigated by the project maintainers
3. **Resolution**: Appropriate action will be taken within 7 days
4. **Follow-up**: You will be informed of the resolution

### Consequences

Consequences for violating the Code of Conduct may include:

- **Warning**: A private written warning from project leadership
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from project spaces
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from project spaces
- **Public Statement**: Public announcement of the violation (in severe cases)
- **Legal Action**: Involvement of law enforcement for illegal behavior

### Appeal Process

If you believe you have been unfairly accused or punished:

1. **Submit Appeal**: Send a written appeal to max@9pros.com
2. **Review**: The appeal will be reviewed by a different maintainer
3. **Decision**: A final decision will be made within 7 days
4. **Communication**: You will be informed of the final decision

## Community Guidelines

### Communication

#### Be Respectful
- Use inclusive language
- Avoid making assumptions about others
- Respect different communication styles
- Practice active listening

#### Be Constructive
- Focus on ideas, not people
- Provide specific, actionable feedback
- Acknowledge good points and contributions
- Offer solutions, not just problems

#### Be Patient
- Remember that everyone has different experience levels
- Allow time for questions and learning
- Provide helpful explanations
- Be understanding of different time zones and schedules

### Collaboration

#### Working Together
- Welcome newcomers and help them get started
- Share knowledge and resources
- Collaborate on solutions rather than competing
- Give credit where credit is due

#### Code Contributions
- Review code thoughtfully and constructively
- Explain the reasoning behind your suggestions
- Be open to feedback on your own work
- Follow project standards and guidelines

#### Documentation
- Write clear, comprehensive documentation
- Keep documentation up to date
- Use inclusive language in all documentation
- Provide examples and use cases

### Learning and Growth

#### Continuous Improvement
- Be open to learning new techniques and approaches
- Share what you've learned with others
- Encourage experimentation and innovation
- Learn from mistakes and help others do the same

#### Mentorship
- Offer to mentor newcomers
- Seek mentorship when you need help
- Create a supportive learning environment
- Celebrate learning achievements

## Specific Guidelines

### Issues and Pull Requests

#### Reporting Issues
- Search existing issues before creating new ones
- Use clear, descriptive titles
- Provide detailed information and reproduction steps
- Include relevant logs, screenshots, or examples
- Be patient while waiting for responses

#### Submitting Pull Requests
- Follow the contribution guidelines
- Write clear commit messages
- Include tests for new functionality
- Update documentation as needed
- Respond to review feedback promptly

### Code Reviews

#### Giving Reviews
- Be constructive and specific
- Focus on the code, not the author
- Explain the reasoning behind your suggestions
- Acknowledge good work and clever solutions
- Be open to discussion and compromise

#### Receiving Reviews
- Listen to feedback with an open mind
- Ask for clarification if needed
- Explain your reasoning when you disagree
- Thank reviewers for their time and input
- Learn from the feedback to improve your skills

### Discussions and Community Spaces

#### General Discussion
- Stay on topic in focused discussions
- Use appropriate channels for different topics
- Be respectful of others' time and attention
- Help keep discussions productive and welcoming

#### Conflict Resolution
- Address disagreements privately when possible
- Focus on finding mutually acceptable solutions
- Involve maintainers if needed
- Remember that we're all working toward the same goals

## Project Leadership Responsibilities

### Leadership Commitment

Project leaders are responsible for:

- **Clarifying** the standards of acceptable behavior
- **Enforcing** the Code of Conduct fairly and consistently
- **Leading by example** in their own behavior
- **Creating** a welcoming and inclusive environment
- **Providing** support and guidance to community members
- **Addressing** violations promptly and appropriately

### Maintainer Expectations

Maintainers should:

- **Model** positive behavior and communication
- **Respond** to reports and concerns promptly
- **Make** decisions based on community well-being
- **Communicate** decisions clearly and respectfully
- **Seek** input from other maintainers when needed
- **Continuously** improve their understanding of inclusive practices

### Accountability

Project leaders are held to the same standards as all community members. If a project leader violates the Code of Conduct, they will:

- **Be subject** to the same consequences as other community members
- **Have** their actions reviewed by other maintainers
- **Face** appropriate consequences based on the violation
- **Receive** support and guidance to improve their behavior
